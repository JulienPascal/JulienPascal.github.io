Spatial Equilibrium and Commuting Costs

Introduction

  • The “Forfait Toutes Zones” (FTZ) entered into force on September 2015 in the Paris area
  • The costs of using public transports decreased for workers living in the outskirts of Paris (zones 3-5)
  • Using the spatial discontinuity generated by the reform, I analyze the links between commuting costs and local employment outcomes

Data

Data Sources

  • Monthly # of workers registered to Pole Emploi at the municipality level: Pole Emploi
  • Demographic and economics characteristics of municipalities and IRIS: INSEE
  • House price index at the municipality level: Chambre des Notaires de Paris
  • Fare area at the IRIS level: Vianavigo
  • Behavior of travelers on the public transport network: STIF

Econometrics

Main specification

$$y_{it} = \alpha_i + \gamma_t + \beta \times \delta_{i,t} + \boldsymbol{\eta} \boldsymbol{x_{i,t}}^{‘} + \varepsilon_{i,t}$$

  • $\alpha_i$: municipality fixed effects
  • $\gamma_t$: time fixed effects
  • $\delta_{i,t}$: indicator = 1 if treated and post-reform
  • $\boldsymbol{x_{i,t}}$: control variables
  • $\varepsilon_{i,t}$: error term

Other spefications

Time-varying impacts

$$ y_{it} = \alpha_i + \gamma_t + \sum_{y=2015}^{2019} \beta_{y} \times \delta_{i,t} \times \delta_{y} + \boldsymbol{\eta} \boldsymbol{x_{i,t}}^{‘} + \varepsilon_{i,t} $$

Different “intensity of treatment”

$$ y_{it} = \alpha_i + \gamma_t + \beta \times z_{i,t} + \boldsymbol{\eta} \boldsymbol{x_{i,t}}^{‘} + \varepsilon_{i,t} $$

$$ y_{it} = \alpha_i + \gamma_t + \sum_{y=2015}^{2019} \beta_{y} \times z_{i,t} \times \delta_{y} + \boldsymbol{\eta} \boldsymbol{x_{i,t}}^{‘} + \varepsilon_{i,t} $$

Spatial RDD

  • For results to be valid, the common trend assumption needs to hold
  • To limit the impact of unobservable characteristics on local employment outcomes, I select municipalities close to the border separating fare areas (zones 2 and 3 in the baseline)

Model

Overview

  • Partial equilibrium model inspired by Brueckner et al. (1999), Postel-Vinay and Robin (2002), Wasmer and Zenou (2002)
  • Rationalize why a decrease in commuting costs may increase local employment

Conclusion

  • The “Forfait Toutes Zones” (FTZ) decreased commuting costs for workers living in the outskirts of Paris (zones 3-5)
  • Using the spatial discontinuity generated by the reform, I find that the FTZ created jobs for long-term unemployed workers in zones 3-5
